TEST: VITAMIN D (25-OH Vitamin D)
UNIT: ng/mL
DEFICIENT: Below 20
INSUFFICIENT: 20 - 29
NORMAL: 30 - 100
TOXIC: Above 100
LOW_MEANS: Vitamin D is low. Very common in India due to indoor lifestyle. May cause bone weakness, fatigue, muscle pain, and low immunity.
HIGH_MEANS: Vitamin D is too high. May indicate excess supplementation.

TEST: VITAMIN B12 (Cobalamin)
UNIT: pg/mL
DEFICIENT: Below 200
BORDERLINE: 200 - 300
NORMAL: 300 - 900
LOW_MEANS: Vitamin B12 is low. Very common in vegetarians. May cause fatigue, weakness, numbness in hands and feet, memory issues, and anemia.
HIGH_MEANS: Vitamin B12 is high. Usually harmless but discuss with doctor.

TEST: FOLATE / FOLIC ACID
UNIT: ng/mL
NORMAL: 3.1 - 17.5
LOW_MEANS: Folate is low. May cause megaloblastic anemia and neural tube defects in pregnancy. Common with poor diet or alcohol use.

TEST: SERUM IRON
UNIT: mcg/dL
NORMAL_MALE: 65 - 175
NORMAL_FEMALE: 50 - 170
LOW_MEANS: Serum iron is low. May indicate iron deficiency anemia or poor diet.
HIGH_MEANS: Serum iron is high. May indicate iron overload or liver disease.

TEST: FERRITIN
UNIT: ng/mL
NORMAL_MALE: 24 - 336
NORMAL_FEMALE: 11 - 307
LOW_MEANS: Ferritin is low. Iron stores are depleted. Early sign of iron deficiency even before anemia develops.
HIGH_MEANS: Ferritin is high. May indicate inflammation, infection, or iron overload.

TEST: TIBC (Total Iron Binding Capacity)
UNIT: mcg/dL
NORMAL: 250 - 370
LOW_MEANS: TIBC is low. May indicate iron overload or chronic disease.
HIGH_MEANS: TIBC is high. Indicates iron deficiency — body is trying to absorb more iron.

TEST: TRANSFERRIN SATURATION
UNIT: %
NORMAL: 20 - 50
LOW_MEANS: Transferrin saturation is low. Confirms iron deficiency.
HIGH_MEANS: Transferrin saturation is high. May indicate iron overload.

TEST: CALCIUM
UNIT: mg/dL
NORMAL: 8.5 - 10.5
LOW_MEANS: Calcium is low. May cause muscle cramps, numbness, weak bones.
HIGH_MEANS: Calcium is high. May indicate hyperparathyroidism or excess vitamin D.

TEST: SODIUM
UNIT: mEq/L
NORMAL: 136 - 145
LOW_MEANS: Sodium is low. May cause nausea, headache, confusion, seizures in severe cases.
HIGH_MEANS: Sodium is high. May indicate dehydration.

TEST: POTASSIUM
UNIT: mEq/L
NORMAL: 3.5 - 5.1
LOW_MEANS: Potassium is low. May cause muscle weakness, cramps, irregular heartbeat.
HIGH_MEANS: Potassium is high. Risk of dangerous heart rhythm problems.

TEST: MAGNESIUM
UNIT: mg/dL
NORMAL: 1.7 - 2.3
LOW_MEANS: Magnesium is low. May cause muscle cramps, anxiety, irregular heartbeat.
HIGH_MEANS: Magnesium is high. May indicate kidney problems.
