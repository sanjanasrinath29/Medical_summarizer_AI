CONDITION: LOW HEMOGLOBIN / ANEMIA
FOODS TO EAT:
- Spinach, kale, broccoli — rich in iron and folate
- Red meat, chicken, liver — high in heme iron absorbed easily
- Lentils, chickpeas, kidney beans — plant based iron
- Tofu and soybeans — good iron source for vegetarians
- Pumpkin seeds, sesame seeds — iron rich snacks
- Dates, raisins, prunes — natural iron sources
- Beetroot — improves blood count
- Pomegranate — rich in iron
- Vitamin C foods like oranges, lemon, amla — help absorb iron better
FOODS TO AVOID:
- Tea and coffee right after meals — block iron absorption
- Processed and junk food
LIFESTYLE:
- Cook in iron cast cookware
- Eat vitamin C with every iron rich meal

CONDITION: HIGH GLUCOSE / PREDIABETES / DIABETES
FOODS TO EAT:
- Leafy greens like spinach and methi
- Whole grains like brown rice, oats, ragi
- Legumes like lentils, rajma, chana
- Nuts like almonds and walnuts in small quantities
- Bitter gourd (karela) — helps regulate blood sugar
- Fenugreek seeds soaked overnight
- Berries — low sugar high fiber
FOODS TO AVOID:
- White rice, white bread, maida
- Sugary drinks and packaged juices
- Deep fried foods
LIFESTYLE:
- Walk 30 minutes after every meal
- Eat small frequent meals
- Never skip breakfast

CONDITION: HIGH CHOLESTEROL / HIGH LDL
FOODS TO EAT:
- Oats — reduces LDL
- Fatty fish like salmon and mackerel
- Flaxseeds and chia seeds
- Olive oil instead of refined oil
- Nuts like walnuts and almonds in moderation
- Garlic — reduces cholesterol
- Beans and legumes
FOODS TO AVOID:
- Red meat and full fat dairy
- Fried foods and trans fats
- Packaged chips and cookies
LIFESTYLE:
- Exercise at least 5 days a week
- Quit smoking

CONDITION: HIGH TRIGLYCERIDES
FOODS TO EAT:
- Fatty fish for omega 3
- Flaxseeds and chia seeds
- Leafy vegetables
- Whole grains
FOODS TO AVOID:
- Alcohol raises triglycerides significantly
- Sugary foods and refined carbs
- Fruit juice high in fructose
LIFESTYLE:
- Lose excess weight
- Exercise regularly

CONDITION: HIGH URIC ACID / GOUT
FOODS TO EAT:
- Plenty of water at least 3 litres daily
- Cherries reduce uric acid
- Low fat dairy
- Vitamin C rich foods
FOODS TO AVOID:
- Red meat and organ meats
- Shellfish
- Alcohol especially beer
- Sugary drinks with fructose

CONDITION: HIGH CREATININE / KIDNEY ISSUES
FOODS TO EAT:
- Plenty of water
- Cauliflower, cabbage, onions, garlic
- Egg whites
- Berries
FOODS TO AVOID:
- Excess protein especially red meat
- Excess salt
LIFESTYLE:
- Stay well hydrated
- Control blood pressure and diabetes

CONDITION: HIGH TSH / HYPOTHYROIDISM
FOODS TO EAT:
- Iodine rich foods like iodised salt and seafood
- Selenium rich foods like brazil nuts and eggs
- Zinc rich foods like pumpkin seeds
FOODS TO AVOID:
- Raw cabbage and broccoli in excess
- Soy products in excess
LIFESTYLE:
- Take thyroid medication on empty stomach

CONDITION: LOW WBC / WEAK IMMUNITY
FOODS TO EAT:
- Citrus fruits for vitamin C
- Garlic and ginger
- Turmeric
- Yogurt and probiotics
- Almonds for vitamin E
FOODS TO AVOID:
- Alcohol suppresses immune system
- Excess sugar
LIFESTYLE:
- Get 7 to 8 hours of sleep
- Manage stress
CONDITION: LOW VITAMIN D
FOODS TO EAT:
- Fatty fish like salmon, mackerel, sardines
- Egg yolks
- Fortified milk and cereals
- Mushrooms exposed to sunlight
LIFESTYLE:
- Get 15-20 minutes of morning sunlight daily
- Best time is 8am to 10am
- Expose arms and legs not just face
- Supplement if doctor recommends

CONDITION: LOW VITAMIN B12
FOODS TO EAT:
- Meat, chicken, fish — best sources
- Eggs and dairy — good for vegetarians
- Fortified cereals and plant milk
- Nutritional yeast
NOTE: B12 is almost only found in animal products. Strict vegetarians and vegans are at high risk. Supplementation is often necessary.
LIFESTYLE:
- Get tested every 6 months if vegetarian
- B12 injections may be needed for severe deficiency

CONDITION: LOW IRON / LOW FERRITIN
FOODS TO EAT:
- Red meat and organ meats like liver
- Spinach, lentils, chickpeas
- Pumpkin seeds, sesame seeds
- Fortified cereals
- Combine with Vitamin C for better absorption
FOODS TO AVOID:
- Tea and coffee block iron absorption
- Calcium rich foods at same time as iron foods
LIFESTYLE:
- Cook in iron cookware
- Take iron supplements on empty stomach if prescribed

CONDITION: LOW CALCIUM
FOODS TO EAT:
- Milk, curd, paneer, cheese
- Ragi (finger millet) — excellent calcium source
- Sesame seeds, almonds
- Green leafy vegetables
- Fortified foods
NOTE: Vitamin D is needed to absorb calcium. Low Vitamin D often leads to low calcium.

CONDITION: LOW POTASSIUM
FOODS TO EAT:
- Bananas, oranges, avocado
- Potatoes, sweet potatoes
- Spinach, beans, lentils
- Coconut water
FOODS TO AVOID:
- Excess salt reduces potassium
