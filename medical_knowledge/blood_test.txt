TEST: HEMOGLOBIN (Hb)
UNIT: g/dL
NORMAL_MALE: 13.5 - 17.5
NORMAL_FEMALE: 12.0 - 15.5
NORMAL_CHILDREN: 11.0 - 16.0
LOW_MEANS: Hemoglobin is low. This may indicate anemia, iron deficiency, or blood loss.
HIGH_MEANS: Hemoglobin is high. May indicate dehydration or a blood disorder.

TEST: RED BLOOD CELLS (RBC)
UNIT_OLD: million cells/mcL
UNIT_MODERN: 10^12/L (same numeric value as million cells/mcL)
NORMAL_MALE: 4.5 - 5.9
NORMAL_FEMALE: 4.1 - 5.1
NOTE: Whether unit is million cells/mcL or 10^12/L the normal range numbers are the same (4.5-5.9 for male)
LOW_MEANS: RBC count is low. May indicate anemia or nutritional deficiency.
HIGH_MEANS: RBC count is high. May indicate dehydration or lung disease.

TEST: WHITE BLOOD CELLS (WBC) / TOTAL LEUCOCYTE COUNT (TLC)
UNIT_OLD: cells/mcL — NORMAL: 4500 - 11000
UNIT_MODERN: 10^9/L — NORMAL: 4.0 - 11.0
NOTE: 4500 cells/mcL = 4.5 x 10^9/L. Both are the same. If value is around 4-11 use 10^9/L range. If value is around 4000-11000 use cells/mcL range.
LOW_MEANS: WBC is low. May indicate viral infection or bone marrow issue.
HIGH_MEANS: WBC is high. May indicate infection, inflammation, or leukemia.

TEST: PLATELETS / PLATELET COUNT
UNIT_OLD: cells/mcL — NORMAL: 150000 - 400000
UNIT_MODERN: 10^9/L — NORMAL: 150 - 400
NOTE: 150000 cells/mcL = 150 x 10^9/L. Both are the same. If value is around 150-400 use 10^9/L range. If value is around 150000-400000 use cells/mcL range.
LOW_MEANS: Platelets are low. Risk of bleeding. May indicate dengue or other conditions.
HIGH_MEANS: Platelets are high. Risk of clotting.

TEST: HEMATOCRIT (HCT) / PACKED CELL VOLUME (PCV)
UNIT: %
NORMAL_MALE: 41 - 53
NORMAL_FEMALE: 36 - 46
LOW_MEANS: Hematocrit is low. Consistent with anemia.
HIGH_MEANS: Hematocrit is high. May indicate dehydration.

TEST: MCV (MEAN CORPUSCULAR VOLUME)
UNIT: fL
NORMAL: 80 - 100
LOW_MEANS: MCV is low. Red blood cells are smaller than normal. May indicate iron deficiency anemia.
HIGH_MEANS: MCV is high. Red blood cells are larger than normal. May indicate B12 or folate deficiency.

TEST: MCH (MEAN CORPUSCULAR HEMOGLOBIN)
UNIT: pg
NORMAL: 27 - 33
LOW_MEANS: MCH is low. Red blood cells carry less hemoglobin than normal. May indicate iron deficiency.
HIGH_MEANS: MCH is high. May indicate macrocytic anemia.

TEST: MCHC (MEAN CORPUSCULAR HEMOGLOBIN CONCENTRATION)
UNIT: g/dL
NORMAL: 31.5 - 34.5
LOW_MEANS: MCHC is low. Red blood cells have less hemoglobin concentration. Consistent with iron deficiency anemia.
HIGH_MEANS: MCHC is high. May indicate hereditary spherocytosis.

TEST: NEUTROPHILS
UNIT: %
NORMAL: 40 - 75
LOW_MEANS: Neutrophils are low. Reduced ability to fight bacterial infections.
HIGH_MEANS: Neutrophils are high. May indicate bacterial infection or inflammation.

TEST: LYMPHOCYTES
UNIT: %
NORMAL: 20 - 45
LOW_MEANS: Lymphocytes are low. Weakened immune response.
HIGH_MEANS: Lymphocytes are high. May indicate viral infection.

TEST: EOSINOPHILS
UNIT: %
NORMAL: 1 - 6
HIGH_MEANS: Eosinophils are high. May indicate allergy or parasitic infection.

TEST: MONOCYTES
UNIT: %
NORMAL: 2 - 10
HIGH_MEANS: Monocytes are high. May indicate chronic infection or inflammation.

TEST: BASOPHILS
UNIT: %
NORMAL: 0 - 1
HIGH_MEANS: Basophils are high. May indicate allergic reaction or inflammation.

TEST: ESR (ERYTHROCYTE SEDIMENTATION RATE)
UNIT: mm/hr
NORMAL_MALE: Below 15
NORMAL_FEMALE: Below 20
HIGH_MEANS: ESR is high. Indicates inflammation or infection somewhere in the body.

TEST: TLC (TOTAL LEUCOCYTE COUNT)
UNIT_MODERN: 10^9/L — NORMAL: 4.0 - 10.0
UNIT_OLD: cells/mcL — NORMAL: 4000 - 10000
NOTE: Same test as WBC. If value is 4-10 it is in 10^9/L. If value is 4000-10000 it is in cells/mcL.
LOW_MEANS: TLC is low. Reduced immunity.
HIGH_MEANS: TLC is high. May indicate infection or inflammation.
